---
name: Handstand Hold
summary: Static handstand against a wall or free — shoulder strength, stability and full-body tension.
instructions:
  - Kneel in front of an open wall, hands shoulder-width, fingertips about a hand's width from the wall.
  - Kick one leg up, the other follows — heels rest lightly against the wall.
  - Hold the body as a straight line — hips and chest stacked over the hands, glutes braced.
  - Lower back down before the shoulders fatigue.
tips:
  - 'Beginners: build pike push-ups and wall walks first, then start practising the hold.'
  - Press the shoulders actively "into the ears" — no sunken position.
---
