---
name: Dynamic Warm-up
summary: Moving warm-up before training — primes joints, muscles and the nervous system.
instructions:
  - 5-10 minutes of easy movement (jog, jumping jacks, jump rope) to raise heart rate.
  - Then targeted mobilisation of the joints you're about to load (hips, shoulders, ankles).
  - Use large ranges of motion but no end-range holds — swings like leg pendulums.
  - Finish with a few light working sets before the real session begins.
tips:
  - Before strength or jumping always go dynamic, never static — static stretching dulls performance.
  - Extend the warm-up in winter; shorten it in hot weather.
