---
name: Step-ups
summary: Step up onto an elevation — single-leg extension with high carry-over to daily life.
instructions:
  - Stand in front of a stable elevation (bench, step).
  - Place one foot completely on the elevation — heel centred on the surface.
  - Drive up through that heel until the back leg is airborne; pause briefly.
  - Step back down under control, repeating on the same leg or alternating.
tips:
  - Pick a height where the front knee bends to about 90° — otherwise momentum takes over.
  - Don't push off the back leg — the stimulus has to come from the working (front) leg.
